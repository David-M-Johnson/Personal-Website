I am a senior studying engineering physics at Cornell University. My interests are in plasma physics, computational science, fusion devices, and astrophysics. I also enjoy software development, writing, and outdoor adventures.

Two years ago, I became interested in fusion energy because of its potential to provide clean, inexpensive power with readily accessible fuel that could reduce geopolitical conflict over natural resources.

Since the fall of 2023, I have researched under several professors, including Drs. Bruce R. Kusse, Jack D. Hare, and Jeremy Chittenden. You can read more about my experience in my CV or by browsing my past and ongoing projects.

After graduation, I plan to continue plasma research or work for a startup. During this time, I hope to decide whether to pursue graduate school and, if so, whether my work would be computational, experimental, or something entirely new.

I also enjoy adventuring in the great outdoors. I wear many hats for Cornell Outdoor Education. This summer, I taught a tree climbing class in the California Redwoods!

I also love to tinker. There is something uniquely rewarding about building things, whether in the machine shop or on a keyboard. If you have an idea for something to build, please let me know. I would love to hear about your projects and potentially work together!

Much of my free time is spent reading and writing. I read anything trusted friends suggest. Right now, I am especially enjoying philosophical fiction, spy novels, and history of science. I also write about culture, literature, philosopy, and theology. I am the senior editor of Claritas, and my writing has been featured in Mere Orthodoxy.
