Magnetic Reconnection of Dual Exploder

Magnetic Reconnection—the process where magnetic field lines break and reconnect, changing magnetic topology—is a fundamental process responsible for solar flares, auroras, and other astrophysical phenomena. Alongside two other undergrads, Jonathan Lee and Mandy Chen, I helped Dr. Jack Hare simulate and analyze the magnetic reconnection of two exploding wire arrays. We compared results to an experimental campaign done on MAIZE by Dr. Hare and Thomas Varnish.

Gas Puff Release Mechanism

In the summer of 2024, I developed a gas release mechanism for exploding gas puff Z-pinches. I wrote a script to simulate the explosion dynamics using the snowplow model to optimize the geometry, designed the part to be compact and veristile, and fabricated it using mill, lathe, and other machine tools. It is currently being used for experiments on COBRA.

HPC Job Status Monitor

This summer, I made an HPC Job Monitor, a Python tool to track the status of HPC jobs through SSH in a GUI. The app checks in the status of you jobs at a specificed interval and returns the latest! It also keeps track of completed jobs so you have a log, and when you reopen it, it picks up from where it left off. Unfortunetly, after two weeks, Imperial College blocked authentication via Paramiko (the python library I was using). I switched the program to use "keyboard-interactive" authentication, and now it works again! We'll see how long it takes Imperial to prevent this method.

Solar Panel Frequency-Response

I examined effect of light wavelength on solar panel efficiency. Sunlight is broadband—it contains a wide range of wavelength, and optimizing solar panels for a given light profile could increase energy generation. The most challenging component of this project was defining "efficiency." I accounted for variables like lux meter frequency-response and the wavelength-energy relation.
